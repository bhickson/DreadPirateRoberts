Wedding Details

Location

https://delacour-ranch.com
Google Maps

We are renting a portion of the ranch on the west side of the road, across the road from the main ranch. The owners are incredibly welcoming, and encourage everyone to walk through the main ranch grounds, feeding carrots to the horses, walking along the creek trail, and smelling the lavender. It is conveniently located about 15 minutes from Lone Pine, so we hope that everyone staying in town will spend as much time with us at the ranch as possible.

Accomodations

The ranch is our main hangout site for the weekend, and is where most of the party will be staying. Ben and Kelly are sleeping onsite. There are three small cabins, reserved for the families with the newest/tiniest humans and Kelly’s folks. There is plenty of room for camping, with flat shady spots for setting up tents. There is a bathhouse with two toilets and a shower, with sinks and running water, as well as a bathroom and small kitchen in Kelly’s folks’ cabin that we’re sure they’ll be happy to share. There is also an outdoor sink with running water. For those staying in the three bungalows onsite (Wil and Julie; Katherine and Sean; Daylin and Antonio): please bring your own bedding and towels (the ranch is not providing these during the pandemic). Also know that the owners are extensively cleaning all spaces, indoors and out, between parties. We will be providing LOTS of cleaning and sanitizing supplies as well.

Health and Safety

We know it’s impossible to know how everyone will feel about being in the presence of new people when that weekend rolls around. There are many tables and lovely spots for gathering and spending time safely outdoors. Weather permitting, we will be able to spend the entire weekend outdoors. We will provide plenty of soap and sanitizer. Ben and Kelly will be safely isolating two weeks prior to the event, and are excited to hug whoever feels equally safe to do so.

Food

We have a large outdoor kitchen that is screened in with plenty of room for coolers and camp stoves, and comes ready with a two-burner stove with a full tank of propane. There is also an outdoor gas grill. The survey will help us determine head counts for meals; from there we will be making catering decisions and tapping folks to help with providing necessary gear and helping with cooking.

Schedule

We have the ranch rented for three nights, Friday September 18th - Monday September 21. Ben and Kelly arrive Friday and everyone else is welcome to as well. Ceremony will be on Sunday morning; specific times are TBD, but we anticipate needing everyone ready to go by 9am. Group photos will take place after the ceremony.

Dogs

There will be three dogs milling about and being awesome; you’re welcome.

Ceremony

We are hoping to have the ceremony about 13 miles up the (graded dirt) road from the ranch, in a lovely meadow location with views of the high Sierra and the East side below. Ben and Kelly will take some photos in the Alabama hills first thing in the morning, giving everyone else time to get ready, and meet you all up there. The ceremony will be short enough to plan on standing around, but we will have enough chairs floating around all weekend that can be hauled up for comfort.

Weather

Keep an eye on the weather as it gets closer of course, but it’ll be mid-late September in the Sierra so plan on cool mornings and nights, with potentially warm daytime temps. Layers will certainly be advised for the morning ceremony.
